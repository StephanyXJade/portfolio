DAVID RUMSEY WEBSITE

The David Rumsey Map Collection is a website that features an extensive collection of historical maps, globes, and atlases. The collection is named after David Rumsey, a map collector and philanthropist who has amassed a significant number of rare and unique maps over the years. The website serves as a platform to share these maps with the public and provide access to a wide range of cartographic materials.

BACKGROUND

The David Rumsey Map Collection is an invaluable resource for researchers, historians, educators, and anyone interested in cartography's rich history and visual representation of the world. Users can frequently zoom in on map details, providing a closer look at intricate cartographic designs and historical features.

PROBLEM

Navigating the website proves challenging due to its extensive content and large image size, making it difficult for users to locate the specific map they seek. It takes more than three clicks to find desired content and over five to return to the preview section. In response, my objective was to streamline the layout, ensuring simplicity while maintaining alignment with branding, guidelines, and user-friendly design principles.

WEBSITE COLOURS

Primary colors

The colour combination of light brown and light blue chosen for my design express an enjoyable and simple look and feel. The geographical and natural features of the maps are represented by light brown, which is generally associated with earthy tones, expressing a sense of foundation and stability. Light blue, on the other hand, represents the navigational qualities of the maps, as it is a symbol of clean skies and water evoking calm and ease of movement. I consider that light brown and light blue colours work perfect together to produce a balanced and user-friendly design, allowing users to easily explore and navigate the map's information.
